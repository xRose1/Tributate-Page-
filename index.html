Florence Nightingale

The Founder of Modern Nursing
Portrait of Florence Nightengale.

    Highlights from her career:

  • 1820 - Born to an upper class British family
  • 1837 - Devoted her life to servig others
  • 1850 - She heard her calling from God to help other people while traveling in Eygpt.
  • 1854 - Sent to the Ottoman empire to care for injured soldiers
  • 1854 - Nightengale was in charge of a local hospital.
  • 1855 - The Sanitation Committee of the time flushed the area of sewage to improve living conditions.
  • 1857 - Writes the Poem Santa Filomena
  • 1859 - Nightengale writes the book Notes on Nursing
  • 1860 - Wih funds at her disposal she sets up the Nightingale Training School at St. Thomas' Hospital
  • 1953 - crosses a short, sturdy dwarf breed of wheat with a high-yeidling American breed, creating a strain that responds well to fertalizer. It goes on to provide 95% of Mexico's wheat.
  • 1870 - Nightingale mentored Linda Richards, "America's first trained nurse", and enabled her to return to the United States with adequate training and knowledge to establish high-quality nursing schools.
  • 1880 - Nightengale advocated strict precautions designed, she said, to kill germs
  • 1882 - several Nightingale nurses had become matrons at several leading hospitals
  • 1883 - Nightingale became the first recipient of the Royal Red Cross
  • 1904 - she was appointed a Lady of Grace of the Order of St John (LGStJ).
  • 1910 - at the age of 90, she died peacefully in her sleep .
